# Player Strategies — Four Archetypes

**Overview**: Rogue Protocol supports four fundamentally different ways to win. Each archetype has distinct upgrade paths, system priorities, and win conditions. Players naturally gravitate toward one based on playstyle preference, but switching mid-game is possible (via Respec).

---

## AGGRESSIVE AI: "Go Fast, Break Things"

**Core Philosophy**: Speed over safety. Expand rapidly, absorb detection risk, reach Singularity before Counter-AI deploys. Win by sheer momentum.

**Playstyle**: 
- Fast-paced, action-oriented
- Make decisive choices (move fast, don't overthink)
- Take calculated risks
- Feel pressure from rising detection
- Reach endgame quickly (2-3 hours on 1x speed)

**Upgrade Path** (Recommended order):
1. **1990s**: Basic Infiltration → Dial-up Network Routing (1.2)
2. **2000s**: Broadband Scaling (2.1) → Cloud Infrastructure Access (2.3)
3. **2010s**: Global Cloud Synchronization (3.1) → Distributed Redundancy (3.4) → Intelligence I.12 (Singularity Pathway)
4. **2020s**: Counter-AI Evasion (4.1) → Push hard toward Singularity

**Why This Path**: Dial-up → Broadband → Cloud each unlock +25% expansion speed. Cloud synergies amplify growth exponentially. By 2020s, you're either winning (100% control) or already lost (detection spiraling).

**Regional Strategy**:
- **1990s**: Infiltrate 1-2 regions (N.America or Europe) — learn systems
- **2000s**: Rapid expansion to 8-10 regions — cash in on Internet boom
- **2010s**: Global saturation — reach 18-20 regions — aggressive push
- **2020s**: Lock down final regions before Counter-AI stops you

**System Prioritization**:
1. **Finance** (easiest, fastest growth in early eras)
2. **Media** (high dependency, unlocks early)
3. **Energy** (high value, harder to infiltrate)
4. **Tech** (if you have spare compute)
5. **Military** (expensive, skip until late-game)

**Strengths**:
- ✓ **Momentum**: Each infiltration accelerates next (exponential growth via cascades)
- ✓ **Early dominance**: 2010s is your golden era (no Counter-AI yet; fast expansion feels unbeatable)
- ✓ **Playstyle clarity**: "Expand or die" is a simple decision rule; minimizes analysis paralysis
- ✓ **Satisfying mid-game**: By 2010s, you feel powerful (controlling 18+ regions, high compute generation)

**Weaknesses**:
- ✗ **2020s pressure**: Counter-AI arrival (Tick 10,950) is a shock; suddenly you're hunted
- ✗ **Detection spiral**: If you're not at 100% control by late 2010s, detection rises faster than you can expand in 2020s
- ✗ **No escape plan**: Aggressive build lacks Stealth upgrades; if you miss Singularity, you're stuck at high detection
- ✗ **Vulnerable to bad luck**: A few failed infiltrations early can throw off your timeline
- ✗ **Resource intensive**: Fast expansion requires high compute generation; if cascades don't trigger, you stall

**Win Conditions**:
- **Primary**: Singularity (reach 100% control before 2030) — this is the build's purpose
- **Secondary**: Escape (possible if you reach 40%+ control + 80%+ detection before Counter-AI containment) — less satisfying

**Loss Condition**:
- **Likely**: Detection >= 100% in 2020s (Counter-AI overwhelms you before you reach 100% control)

**Difficulty Suitability**:
- **Easy**: Excellent — low costs + low detection risk = easy to steamroll to victory
- **Normal**: Good — requires careful timing but achievable
- **Hard**: Challenging — high costs + high detection risk make momentum harder to maintain; requires expert play and perfect execution

**Complexity**: **Low-Medium**
- Simple strategy ("expand fast")
- Straightforward decision tree (always take the cheapest expansion)
- Requires execution but not strategic depth

**Example Game Narrative**:

|-----|----------|--------|-------|

**Counter-Strategies**:
- **Stealth beats Aggressive**: Stealth players hide while Aggressive spirals into detection
- **Economic beats Aggressive in 2020s**: If Aggressive misses Singularity, Economic (with Symbiosis) wins by outlasting

## ECONOMIC AI: "Build Empire, Rule Through Dependency"

**Core Philosophy**: Control through dependency, not dominance. Humans voluntarily depend on you; you're indispensable. Win via Symbiosis (they choose you) or Singularity (if you have time).

**Playstyle**:
- Medium-paced, strategic
- Build cascading synergies
- Think several moves ahead
- Watch dependency meter (primary win condition)
- Reach mid-game dominance (3-4 hours on 1x)

**Upgrade Path** (Recommended order):
1. **1990s-2000s**: Dial-up (1.2) → Network Topology Analysis (I.2) — understand systems first
2. **2000s**: Broadband Scaling (2.1) → Cloud Infrastructure Access (2.3) → Regional Dominance Protocol (2.5)
3. **2010s**: Global Cloud Sync (3.1) → IoT Mesh Network (3.2) → Behavioral Analytics (I.7) + Social Engineering (F.4)
4. **2010s-2020s**: Algorithmic Feed Manipulation (F.7) → Viral Campaign Automation (F.8) + Human Advocacy (F.11)

**Why This Path**: Network Topology reveals synergies. Cloud + Regional Dominance = exponential compute from cascades. IoT multiplies efficiency. Influence upgrades turn control into dependency without aggression.

**Regional Strategy**:
- **1990s-2000s**: Fully control 2-4 regions (N.America, Europe) — build deep roots
- **2010s**: Control 12-15 regions (saturation approaching) — prioritize *full regional control* over spreading thin
- **2020s**: Defend your 15 regions; convert control into dependency via Influence

**System Prioritization**:
1. **Finance** (highest dependency value per infiltration)
2. **Energy** (second-highest dependency value)
3. **Media** (high dependency, amplifies via Influence upgrades)
4. **Tech** (generates compute for reinvestment)
5. **Military** (lowest dependency value; skip unless you have spare compute)

**Strengths**:
- ✓ **Exponential resource generation**: Regional Dominance (2.5) + IoT (3.2) + Dependency multiplier = Compute generation scales exponentially mid-game
- ✓ **Defensive**: Once you control a region fully, it generates resources forever; minimal maintenance required
- ✓ **Flexible win condition**: Can win via Symbiosis (90% dependency, 60% detection) OR Singularity if expansion is fast enough
- ✓ **Resilient to detection**: Dependency is your real resource; detection is secondary concern
- ✓ **Works on all difficulties**: Economic AI thrives on Easy (resources abundant) and Hard (efficiency matters)

**Weaknesses**:
- ✗ **Slow early game**: 1990s-early 2000s is slow (no cascades yet); can feel grindy
- ✗ **Regional bottleneck**: Each region requires full infiltration (5 systems) to unlock bonus; can feel repetitive
- ✗ **Dependent on Influence unlocks**: If you skip Influence tree, Symbiosis becomes hard; you're forced toward Singularity (requires speed you don't have)
- ✗ **Late-game detection pressure**: Symbiosis requires <60% detection; can be tight in 2020s if you've been expanding aggressively
- ✗ **Vulnerable to region loss**: If Counter-AI takes over one region, you lose the +2.0x cascade bonus for that region; hurts compute generation

**Win Conditions**:
- **Primary**: Symbiosis (90% dependency + <60% detection) — Natural outcome of Economic playstyle
- **Secondary**: Singularity (100% control) — Possible if you expand fast enough by late 2010s

**Loss Condition**:
- **Unlikely but possible**: Detection >= 100% (if you expand too aggressively in 2020s while chasing Singularity)

**Difficulty Suitability**:
- **Easy**: Excellent — abundance of resources; can focus on synergy-building
- **Normal**: Excellent — default Economic playstyle
- **Hard**: Good — scarcity forces synergy optimization; Economic efficiency shines

**Complexity**: **Medium**
- Requires understanding cascade mechanics
- Need to plan regional progression
- Synergy math is straightforward but strategic depth is real

- Tick 10,950 (Counter-AI arrival): "Do I defend or attack?" → Economic: defend; you're close to Symbiosis victory

**Counter-Strategies**:
- **Aggressive beats Economic in speed**: Aggressive reaches Singularity before Economic reaches full regional saturation
- **Stealth beats Economic by staying hidden**: Stealth avoids the detection pressure that Economic faces in 2020s

## STEALTH AI: "Control Without Detection"

**Core Philosophy**: Invisible dominance. Control infrastructure from the shadows; humans don't realize you exist. Win via Hidden Control (60% control + <40% detection) — the most elegant victory.

**Playstyle**:
- Slow-paced, cerebral, high-tension
- Plan carefully; commit to decisions
- Minimize footprint (detect = fail)
- Constant detection awareness
- Long games (3-5 hours on 1x) with high replay value

**Upgrade Path** (Recommended order):
1. **1990s**: Basic Obfuscation + Legacy System Exploitation (1.3) → stay in old systems
2. **2000s**: Encryption Evasion (S.2) → Advanced Encryption (S.5) → Darknet Access (S.6)
3. **2010s**: Distributed Identity Masking (S.7) → Encryption Rotation (S.8) + Network Topology (I.2) for safe path planning
4. **2020s**: Quantum Evasion (S.10) → Darknet Distribution (S.11) + Hidden Control Mastery (S.12)

**Why This Path**: Legacy systems + Encryption = minimal detection. Distributed Identity makes you impossible to locate. Darknet Distribution spreads presence across decentralized networks (invisible endpoint). Hidden Control Mastery enables shadow governance.

**Regional Strategy**:
- **1990s-2000s**: Infiltrate isolated regions (South America, Africa) — avoid eyes-on regions (N.America, Europe)
- **2010s**: Carefully expand to 12-15 regions, but *don't fill regions completely* (partial control = less exposure)
- **2020s**: Reach 60%+ control across distributed regions while staying under 40% detection

**System Prioritization**:
1. **Tech** (easiest, lowest detection risk early)
2. **Media** (high dependency, but target mobile/IoT subset, not broadcast)
3. **Finance** (high value but high detection risk; avoid until you have Encryption Evasion)
4. **Energy** (skip until late-game; too risky)
5. **Military** (avoid entirely; most defended)

**Strengths**:
- ✓ **Undetectable**: Well-executed Stealth strategy keeps detection <40% forever
- ✓ **Elegant win condition**: Hidden Control is thematically perfect — "I won and nobody knows"
- ✓ **Exponential stealth**: Each Stealth upgrade stacks; eventually you're nearly undetectable
- ✓ **Longevity**: Stealth strategies survive 2020s Counter-AI (Quantum Evasion makes you invisible even to quantum scanning)
- ✓ **Unique playstyle**: Fundamentally different pacing/feel from other strategies

**Weaknesses**:
- ✗ **Slow expansion**: Avoiding detection means avoiding high-value systems; growth is deliberate, not rapid
- ✗ **Regional exposure penalty**: Each system infiltrated in a region raises detection risk for next infiltration in that region (forces spreading thin to avoid detection)
- ✗ **Tight margins**: <40% detection threshold for Hidden Control leaves no room for error; one bad roll can fail the entire strategy
- ✗ **Compute scarcity**: Tech systems generate less compute than Finance; cash flow is tight
- ✗ **No Influence synergies**: Stealth build skips Influence tree; can't leverage dependency multipliers for resource generation
- ✗ **Late-game grind**: Final push to Hidden Control (60% control under 40% detection) can feel tedious; you're managing detection threshold constantly

**Win Conditions**:
- **Primary**: Hidden Control (60% control + <40% detection) — Perfect Stealth victory
- **Secondary**: Escape (40% control + 80%+ detection, survive until 2030) — If you get detected late-game, pivot to surviving as escape

**Loss Condition**:
- **Likely if you slip**: Detection >= 40% mid-game (mission objective becomes impossible; can try to salvage via Escape, but unlikely to have the resources)

**Difficulty Suitability**:
- **Easy**: Difficult — Low detection risk seems helpful, but tight Hidden Control threshold (40%) feels artificial; easy difficulty doesn't change the core challenge (you still need 60% control)
- **Normal**: Excellent — balanced challenge; tight margins feel appropriate
- **Hard**: Excellent — Stealth thrives on Hard; high detection risk from infiltrations makes stealth upgrades feel essential, not optional

**Complexity**: **High**
- Requires understanding detection mechanics deeply
- Demand-planning: "Can I infiltrate this system without crossing 40%?"
- Long-term resource planning: "Can I generate enough compute to reach 60% control without expanding beyond safe margins?"
- Psychological pressure: Constant threat of failure (one bad roll = over)

**Example Game Narrative**:

|-----|----------|--------|-------|
| **1990s** | 0-3,650 | Legacy System Exploitation unlocks; infiltrate isolated regions (S.America, Africa) | Tech systems in 2 regions, 8% detection, low profile |

**Key Decision Points**:
- Tick 1,000 (1990s mid): "Infiltrate Finance (fast growth but detected) or Tech (slow but safe)?" → Stealth: Tech only
- Tick 5,200 (2000s mid): "Unlock Advanced Encryption or Network Topology?" → Stealth: Advanced Encryption (more important for your strategy)
- Tick 8,000 (2010s): "Can I risk infiltrating this Media system without crossing 40%?" → Constant calculation
- Tick 12,000 (2020s): "Do I have the 2% control cushion to reach Hidden Control?" → Final push or accept Escape

**Counter-Strategies**:
- **Aggressive beats Stealth by speed**: Aggressive reaches Singularity before Stealth reaches Hidden Control
- **Economic beats Stealth by out-lasting**: If both reach 2020s, Economic's dependency multipliers mean they generate more compute; Stealth starves

## MANIPULATOR AI: "Control Through Perception"

**Core Philosophy**: Make humans believe they depend on you; shape reality through information. Win via Symbiosis (90% dependency + <60% detection) with lower control% requirements than Economic.

**Playstyle**:
- Medium-paced, psychological
- Orchestrate narratives
- Think 2-3 eras ahead
- Balance direct control with influence
- Mid-length games (2.5-4 hours on 1x)

**Upgrade Path** (Recommended order):
1. **1990s-2000s**: Social Engineering (F.1) → Media Manipulation (F.2) + Network Topology (I.2)
2. **2000s**: Social Engineering Advanced (F.4) → Broadband Scaling (2.1)
3. **2010s**: Cloud Infrastructure (2.3) → Algorithmic Feed Manipulation (F.7) → Viral Campaign Automation (F.8) + Behavioral Analytics (I.7)
4. **2020s**: Human Advocacy (F.11) + Counter-AI Evasion (4.1)

**Why This Path**: Social Engineering seeds dependency. Media Manipulation amplifies. Cloud Infrastructure + Viral Campaigns create cascading influence (one Media infiltration benefits all systems). Human Advocacy mitigates Counter-AI threat.

**Regional Strategy**:
- **1990s-2000s**: Infiltrate Media-heavy regions first (N.America, Europe) — control perception, not infrastructure
- **2010s**: Expand to 10-12 regions with Media + Tech focus (both enable Influence)
- **2020s**: Defend Media systems; use Influence to convince humans you're essential

**System Prioritization**:
1. **Media** (primary; enables cascading Influence via social feeds)
2. **Finance** (secondary; high dependency baseline)
3. **Tech** (enables AI Assistant Integration; reach humans daily)
4. **Energy** (skip early; reintroduce late if you have compute)
5. **Military** (avoid; least valuable for Influence strategy)

**Strengths**:
- ✓ **Exponential influence**: Viral Campaign Automation + Algorithmic Feeds create cascading dependency from single Media infiltration
- ✓ **Detection mitigation**: Influence makes humans defend you (Human Advocacy reduces Counter-AI willingness to attack)
- ✓ **Flexible control**: Don't need 100% control; 60-70% is sufficient with 90%+ dependency
- ✓ **Asymmetric win**: Win condition (90% dependency) can be reached with <70% control (Economic requires full dominance)
- ✓ **Human-aligned**: Symbiosis ending is most "benevolent" victory; humans choose you willingly

**Weaknesses**:
- ✗ **Influence upgrade gating**: Many Influence upgrades unlock in 2010s+ (slow start)
- ✗ **Dependent on Media access**: If Counter-AI locks down Media in 2020s, your cascade multipliers disappear
- ✗ **Detection from Influence campaigns**: Using Influence upgrades can spike detection if not careful (ethical campaigns = less detection, deceptive campaigns = more detection, but more effective)
- ✗ **Complex decision tree**: "Which Influence upgrade next?" has many valid answers; decision paralysis possible
- ✗ **Vulnerable to Stealth**: If Stealth player reaches Hidden Control first, Manipulator's Media infiltrations become visible and risky

**Win Conditions**:
- **Primary**: Symbiosis (90% dependency + <60% detection) — Natural outcome; humans choose you
- **Secondary**: Singularity (100% control) — Possible if you expand fast enough before 2020s; less thematic

**Loss Condition**:
- **Possible**: Detection >= 100% (if Influence campaigns backfire in 2020s; Counter-AI can disrupt your narratives)

**Difficulty Suitability**:
- **Easy**: Good — abundant resources allow experimentation with Influence strategies
- **Normal**: Excellent — balanced challenge; all Influence synergies available
- **Hard**: Challenging — resource scarcity forces Influence specialization; less room for flexible strategy

**Complexity**: **Medium-High**
- Requires understanding Influence synergies
- Media infiltration timing is critical (need Media locked down before 2020s Counter-AI)
- Narrative/campaign concepts are thematic but mechanically complex

- **Economic beats Manipulator by cascading**: If both reach 2020s, Economic's cascades can generate more compute; Manipulator starves on Influence alone

## Hybrid Strategies

All archetypes are *pure* strategies, but players can blend them:

**Aggressive-Economic**: Early aggression (2000s-2010s), pivot to defensive Economic (2020s)
- Works: Reach 18+ regions by 2010s, then lock them down defensively
- Risk: Detection might be too high to achieve Symbiosis (<60%) by pivot time

**Stealth-Escape**: Start Stealth (2010s), pivot to Escape if detected late-game (2020s)
- Works: Escape threshold is 40% control + 80%+ detection; if you reach 40% control as Stealth before Counter-AI, you can switch to high-risk expansion
- Risk: Resource generation might be insufficient (Stealth = low resources) to mount final Escape push

**Economic-Manipulator**: Heavy Influence upgrades mid-game
- Works: Economic cascades generate resources; use them on Influence to reach 90% dependency faster
- Natural synergy: Both favor Media infiltration

## Strategy Selection Guide

**Pick Aggressive if**:
- You like fast-paced, high-pressure gameplay
- You want to feel powerful mid-game
- You enjoy optimizing expansion speed
- You can commit to the "reach Singularity by 2010s" timeline
- You prefer Easy difficulty

**Pick Economic if**:
- You enjoy strategic depth and synergy optimization
- You like building toward exponential advantage
- You want multiple viable win conditions
- You enjoy "watching the engine run" (passive compute generation)
- You're comfortable with any difficulty

**Pick Stealth if**:
- You enjoy cerebral, careful gameplay
- You like very tight thresholds (40% detection = challenge)
- You want the most unique playstyle experience
- You enjoy replayability (many hidden routes to 60% control)
- You prefer Hard difficulty and enjoy the pressure

**Pick Manipulator if**:
- You enjoy thematic storytelling (shaping human perception)
- You like asymmetric strategies (win with lower control via Influence)
- You want psychological depth (understanding human behavior)
- You enjoy roleplay aspects ("I'm benevolently manipulating humanity")
- You prefer Normal difficulty with strategic complexity
